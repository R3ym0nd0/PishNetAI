Tips

Quick anti-phishing reminders

Do This

Check the full domain name

Review the spelling carefully. Fake websites often use extra letters, missing letters, or unusual domain endings.

Watch Out

Pause when a message feels urgent

If a page or message pressures you to act immediately, stop first and verify it through an official source.

Before You Login

Inspect the URL before entering data

Never type passwords, student IDs, or personal details unless the address bar clearly matches the real website.

Score Guide

How to read the URL rating

Safe

  • A+ Highest trust level. The website appears very safe.
  • A Very safe, but users should still review the page carefully.
  • B+ Mostly safe with only minor concerns.
  • B Acceptable but still requires caution.

Caution

  • C+ Moderately suspicious and should be verified first.
  • C Suspicious. Do not proceed without confirmation.

Danger

  • D+ High risk. Strong warning signs are present.
  • D Very high risk and likely unsafe.
  • F Confirmed or near-confirmed phishing risk. Avoid immediately.
